# CKM Physical Activity Study
## Cardiometabolic Risk and Exercise Behavior Analysis

Clinical data collection and analysis for an IRB-approved study 
examining physical activity levels among individuals with 
Cardiokidney Metabolic Syndrome (CKM).

**Principal Investigator: Dr. Navin Kaushal, PhD, FAHA**  
Population Health Research Laboratory  
Indiana University Indianapolis, School of Health and Human Sciences

---

## Background

Cardiokidney Metabolic Syndrome (CKM) is a newly recognized syndrome 
by the American Heart Association occurring when an individual has a 
combination of conditions including being overweight plus metabolic 
risk factors (hypertriglyceridemia, hypertension, metabolic syndrome, 
diabetes) or chronic kidney disease.

CKM may be reversible through regular exercise. This study examines 
physical activity levels among CKM individuals to design an effective 
evidence-based exercise program.

## Eligibility Criteria

**Inclusion:**
- Adults 18+ years
- Diagnosis of CKM and/or combination of metabolic risk factors
- Overweight + hypertriglyceridemia (≥135 mg/dL)
- Hypertension, metabolic syndrome, diabetes, or chronic kidney disease

---

## Study Protocol

### What Participants Do
- Complete two online surveys (10 min each) 4 weeks apart
- Wear a physical activity tracker for 2 weeks at study start
- Wear a physical activity tracker for 2 weeks at 4-week follow-up
- Receive/return tracker by mail or in-person pickup

### Data Collected
- Survey responses - health beliefs, barriers, motivators
- Accelerometer data - daily steps, activity intensity, sedentary time
- Demographic information - age, gender, health conditions

## Analysis Plan

### Phase 1 - Descriptive Analysis (Current)
- Participant demographics and baseline characteristics
- Physical activity levels at baseline (accelerometer + self-report)
- Survey response distributions across HBM constructs

### Phase 2 - Comparative Analysis (Upcoming)
- Pre vs post physical activity comparison (Week 1 vs Week 4)
- Subgroup analysis by CKM severity
- Correlation between self-reported and accelerometer-measured activity

### Phase 3 - Intervention Design (Future)
- Evidence-based exercise program recommendations
- Targeting identified barriers and motivators from survey data
